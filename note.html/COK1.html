ACTS OF RIGHTEOUSNESS

Being a human is not easy? it ain't easy but its worth it.
We are called to be righteous, to do good works, to love our neighbors, to forgive those who wrong us, and to live a life that reflects the love of Christ.
But we cannot do this on our own. We need the power of the Holy Spirit to help us to live a life of righteousness. We need to rely on God's grace and mercy to help us to overcome our sinful nature and to live a life that is pleasing to Him.
So let us strive to be faithful in Christ Jesus, to continue in Him, and to live a life of righteousness. Let us not be slaves to sin, but slaves to righteousness. And let us remember that it is only through the power of the Holy Spirit and the grace of God that we can live a life that is pleasing to Him.
Let us also remember that being a slave to righteousness does not mean that we are perfect or that we will never make mistakes. It means that we are committed to living a life that is pleasing to God, even when it is difficult. It means that we are willing to sacrifice our own desires and preferences for the sake of others and for the glory of God.
So let us continue to strive for righteousness, to do good works, and to love our neighbors as ourselves. Let us remember that we are not alone in this journey, but that we have the support and encouragement of our fellow believers and the power of the Holy Spirit to help us along the way. And let us always remember that our ultimate goal is to glorify God and to enjoy Him forever.

MATTHEW 6:2-18

"Be careful not to practice your righteousness in front of others to be seen by them. If you do, you will have no reward from your Father in heaven.
So when you give to the needy, do not announce it with trumpets, as the hypocrites do in the synagogues and on the streets, to be honored by others. Truly I tell you, they have received their reward in full.
But when you give to the needy, do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing, so that your giving may be in secret. Then your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you.
And when you pray, do not be like the hypocrites, for they love to pray standing in the synagogues and on the street corners to be seen by others. Truly I tell you, they have received their reward in full.
But when you pray, go into your room, close the door and pray to your Father,who is unseen. Then your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you.
And when you fast, do not look somber as the hypocrites do, for they disfigure their faces to show others they are fasting. Truly I tell you, they have received their reward in full.
But when you fast, put oil on your head and wash your face, so that it will not be obvious to others that you are fasting, but only to your Father, who is unseen; and your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you."
